# NMEA Output

<p class="lead">FairWinds can broadcast live NMEA 0183 sentences so that external routing tools like QTVLM and Expedition can see your boat as if it were a real instrument feed.</p>

---

## What is NMEA?

NMEA 0183 is the standard protocol that sailing instruments use to talk to each other — the same format your chartplotter, GPS, and wind instruments use on a real boat. By broadcasting NMEA sentences, FairWinds lets external routers treat your virtual boat exactly like a real vessel: they see your position, speed, heading, wind angle, and wind speed updating in real time.

This means you can run a full routing session in QTVLM or Expedition — with your real polars, tide tables, and routing algorithms — driven entirely by your FairWinds race.
