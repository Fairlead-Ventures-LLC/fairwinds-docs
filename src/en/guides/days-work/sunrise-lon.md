# Sunrise Longitude Fix

<p class="lead">Time the moment the sun's lower limb touches the horizon. That time is your longitude.</p>

← Back to [Daily Fixes](/en/guides/days-work/)

---

## When

**±45 minutes around sunrise.** The exact moment varies by your position — use the predicted sunrise time from the Day's Work panel as your watch cue.

## The Recipe

1. Note your predicted sunrise time from the Day's Work panel (based on your current DR)
2. Be at the sextant a few minutes before, horizon clear
3. Open the Sky Tool → **Solar** tab → **Sunrise/Sunset**
4. At the moment the sun's lower limb clears the horizon, record the UTC time
5. Save the longitude fix — it plots a north-south line of position on the chart

## Why

The time of sunrise is predictable from the almanac. If you know the exact UTC moment it occurred, you can work backwards to your longitude. It's the mirror image of the noon sight: noon gives you latitude directly, sunrise/sunset gives you longitude directly.

The geometry is poor for latitude at sunrise (the sun is moving horizontally), which is why this is a **longitude fix only** — but it's a quick, clean one that costs almost no effort.

## In FairWinds

The longitude fix is computed and saved automatically when you record a sunrise observation in the Solar tab. It appears in the Positions panel as a north-south line.

---

*Optional — pairs naturally with the noon sight to give you a full position by midday.*
