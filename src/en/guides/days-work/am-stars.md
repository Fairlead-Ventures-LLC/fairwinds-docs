# AM Twilight Star Fix

<p class="lead">The most accurate fix of the day. Shoot multiple stars at dawn while the horizon is still sharp.</p>

← Back to [Daily Fixes](/en/guides/days-work/)

---

## When

**Dawn to sunrise** — the window when the sky is dark enough to see stars clearly but the horizon is still lit. This is civil twilight. It lasts roughly 20–30 minutes.

## The Recipe

1. Open the Sky Tool before dawn
2. Identify 3–4 stars spread around the compass (aim for 60°+ between them) — see [Picking Stars for a Fix](star-selection/)
3. Shoot each one, recording the time to the second
4. Compute a line of position (LOP) for each sight
5. The intersection of three LOPs is your fix — they should form a small triangle

## Why

Stars give you wide crossing angles that the sun alone cannot. A single sun sight gives one line; three stars give three lines that cross at good angles from all directions. The resulting fix is overdetermined — if one sight is off, the other two will show it. This is the only observation in the Day's Work that doesn't depend on any prior DR.

**A single good star fix resets everything.** If your DR has drifted for days, this snaps you back.

## In FairWinds

Open the Sky Tool → **Star** tab. Shoot 3+ bodies during the twilight window shown in Bowditch's Day's Work panel. Save each LOP, then select them and tap **Fix** to get the crossed position.
